Cloudweave is an AI-powered frame interpolation and video visualization system developed for ISRO‚Äôs problem statement on improving the temporal resolution of satellite imagery from OGC-compatible WMS (Web Map Services). It tackles the challenge of infrequent satellite updates‚Äîtypically one image every 30 minutes‚Äîby generating multiple intermediate frames using deep learning, creating smooth and continuous videos that reveal dynamic weather patterns like cloud motion, cyclones, and storms in near real-time.

Powered by the FILM (Frame Interpolation for Large Motion) model, Cloudweave effectively handles deformable objects such as clouds that may appear or vanish between frames. It fetches GeoTIFF imagery from WMS servers like MOSDAC and BHUVAN, applies noise reduction via the Sobel Operator, and performs recursive AI-based interpolation to produce up to 30√ó more frames. These frames are merged using FFmpeg into seamless videos at one-minute granularity.

The system is fully web-based‚Äîbuilt with Python, PyTorch, OpenCV, and FFmpeg on the backend, and React.js with OpenLayers on the frontend. Deployed on Vercel, it overlays generated videos directly onto interactive maps, providing a GPU-accelerated, open-source alternative to commercial tools like Mapbox.

Cloudweave aligns with ISRO‚Äôs vision of open, self-reliant geospatial innovation.
